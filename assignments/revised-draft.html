Revising Your Draft

After receiving feedback from peer review (or your ASC tutor visit), your next step is to revise your draft with a focus on the ideas and structure of your paper. This means looking at how your analysis is organized, whether your points are fully developed, and how well your evidence supports your claims.

Some questions to guide your revision:

  • Does the overall organization of your paper make sense? Are your ideas presented in a logical order?
  • Are your convention paragraphs fully developed with enough analysis and evidence?
  • Are you using your genre samples effectively to support your points — and using more than one example per convention to show a pattern?
  • Do your paragraphs connect to each other with transitions, or does the paper feel choppy?
  • Does your introduction set up the paper clearly, and does your conclusion bring your analysis together?
  • Did your peer reviewers flag anything that you haven't addressed yet?

This Is a Second Draft, Not a Final Draft

You will have more opportunities to improve this paper. After submitting this revised draft, you will meet with me for an individual conference where I'll give you direct feedback on your essay. At the end of the semester, you will revise this paper one more time as part of your Final Portfolio.

So for this draft, focus on making meaningful revisions to your ideas, organization, flow, and structure. It doesn't need to be perfect — but it should show real progress from your rough draft.

Full Requirements

Your revised draft must meet all of the following:

  • Length: 1300–1600 words (~5–7 pages), not including the Works Cited page.
  • Format: MLA Style.
  • Required topics: Address all required prompts/topics listed under the "Assignment Prompt" section on the Genre Analysis Essay assignment page.
  • Evidence from genre samples: Use at least three writing/video samples from your community to support your points. Include direct quotes and/or screenshots (for visual conventions) as support.
    • For shorter genres, such as Discord messages, I recommend using a series of messages from three separate conversations.
  • Support your claims: Throughout your paper, support your claims using either your genre samples or specific details from your observations in your community.
    • Use more than one sample/example to support each convention, since you are trying to show a trend.
  • In-text citations: Clearly state the username/name of the creator of the post or message you are analyzing.
  • Works Cited page: Include a Works Cited page (MLA Style) with links/references to your samples. See the MLA Formatting & Citations page for more information.
  • AI Statement of Use: A detailed AI Statement of Use is required at the end of your paper. See the AI Statement of Use section below.

AI Statement of Use

Required for All Students

Include a detailed AI Statement of Use at the end of your paper, regardless of whether you used AI or not.

If you did not use GenAI: State that you did not use GenAI in any capacity.

If you used GenAI in any capacity, your statement must include:

  • Which AI tool was used (e.g., ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, etc.)
  • Which pages or paragraphs the AI assisted with
  • What specific tasks the AI helped you with for those sections (e.g., brainstorming, revising for clarity, proofreading)

You may use GenAI to help write your AI Statement of Use, but you are responsible for making sure it is accurate and truthful.
