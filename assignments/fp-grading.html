Final Portfolio Grading

The final portfolio is graded holistically — both the Genre Analysis Essay (Final Draft) and the Final Reflection are considered together as one grade.

Grading Criteria

Grade: A (95%)

Both the Genre Analysis Essay (Final Draft) and the Final Reflection meet all assignment requirements. The essay demonstrates meaningful revision since the revised draft, is well organized, and claims are well supported by evidence from genre samples from your community. The essay is readable, generally proofread, and follows MLA formatting and citation conventions. The Final Reflection is thoughtful and well developed, with well-chosen artifacts that clearly demonstrate the points being made.

Grade: B (85%)

Both items meet assignment requirements, but one area across either the essay or the Final Reflection isn't quite working — for example, a strong essay but a reflection that stays surface-level, or a thoughtful reflection but an essay with uneven organization or thin evidence integration.

Grade: C (75%)

Either both the essay and the Final Reflection have areas that aren't quite working, or one of them has more significant issues. The work meets the basic assignment requirements but doesn't fully deliver on the criteria described above.

Grade: D (65%)

Attempts at both the essay and the Final Reflection are clear, but there are major issues with both. Requirements may be partially met, but significant gaps in revision, reflection depth, organization, evidence use, or other criteria are present across the portfolio.

Grade: E

One of the portfolio items does not meet the assignment requirements or was not submitted — for example, the Final Reflection was not submitted but the Genre Analysis Essay (Final Draft) was and generally meets the criteria, or vice versa.

An E grade will fall between 0% and 55% depending on the extent of the missing or unmet requirements.

No Credit (0%)

The final portfolio was not submitted.

Note: An A portfolio receives a 95%. Scores above 95% are reserved for portfolios that demonstrate exceptional work above and beyond the criteria described above.
