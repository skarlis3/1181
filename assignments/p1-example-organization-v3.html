Example Essay Organization

This is a flexible framework — all of these sections are required, but the order and length can be adapted to fit your community and genre. Some sections may be longer than shown here. For example, you might have four convention paragraphs instead of three, split your genre function into two paragraphs, or use a two-paragraph introduction.

The Hourglass Shape

Your essay works best with a broad → narrow → broad structure:

Broad: Community overview & genre intro
▼
Narrower: Purpose & audience
▼
Narrowest: Convention analysis
▼
Broader: Genre function in community
▼
Broad: Conclusion

Example Outline

1 Introduction (1–2 paragraphs)

Introduce your community, the platform, and the genre you will analyze.

Option A: Two paragraphs

Paragraph 1: Introduce your discourse community and how they communicate on the platform.

Paragraph 2: Describe the different types of posts. Then state which one your essay will focus on (this is your thesis).

Option B: One paragraph

Combine community, platform, and genre intro into one paragraph. Best for communities that don't need a lot of background.

2 Genre Description, Purpose, & Audience (1–3 paragraphs)

  • Provide an overview and description of the genre.
  • Discuss the purpose — why this genre exists in the community.
  • Analyze the audience and how they shape the genre.
These can be one paragraph or 2–3, depending on how much you have to say. The description should come early, but purpose and audience could also work later in the paper.

3 Convention #1 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick one convention (common feature) to analyze.
  • Describe it and use evidence from your samples to support your point.
  • Analyze how it makes the genre effective (or ineffective).

4 Convention #2 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick another convention to analyze.
  • Describe it and use evidence from your samples.
  • Analyze its effectiveness.

5 Convention #3 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick a third convention to analyze.
  • Describe it and use evidence from your samples.
  • Analyze its effectiveness.

6 How the Genre Functions in the Community (1–2 paragraphs)

Pick one or two of the following approaches, or come up with your own:

Context & Circulation

How and where is this genre used? Formal or informal? How is it shared?

Interaction

How do members interact with this genre? What do comments and reactions show?

Values & Norms

What does this genre reveal about the community's values and goals?

Broader Implications

What does this analysis suggest about how genres work in communities generally?

You don't need to cover all of these. Pick what makes sense for your genre and community.

7 Conclusion (1 paragraph)

  • Summarize your key findings from the analysis.
  • Restate how the genre contributes to the goals and functioning of the discourse community.
