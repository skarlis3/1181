Example Essay Organization

This is not the required organization — just an example outline that works well for this assignment. You can adapt it to fit your community and genre.

A Note on Organization

This paper works best with a broad to narrow approach. Think of it as an hourglass:

  • Broad at the start: overview of the community, genres in the community, and the genre you plan to analyze
  • Narrow in the middle: detailed analysis of the genre — audience, purpose, and at least three conventions
  • Broad at the end: how the genre functions in the community, then wrap up in a conclusion

Example Outline

Introduction

Introduction (1–2 paragraphs)

Option A — Two-paragraph intro:

  1. Paragraph 1: Introduce your discourse community and how they communicate on the platform you are focusing on.
  2. Paragraph 2: Introduce the different types of posts in your community. Describe each briefly. Then state which type of post your essay will analyze (this is your thesis).

Option B — One-paragraph intro:

Introduce your discourse community, the platform, and the genre you will analyze — all in one paragraph. Best for communities that don't need a lot of background (e.g., Discord servers).

Body Paragraphs

Genre Description, Purpose, & Audience (1–3 paragraphs)

  • Provide an overview and description of the genre.
  • Discuss the purpose of this genre — why it exists in the community.
  • Analyze the intended audience and the role they play in shaping the genre.
These can be covered in one paragraph or spread across 2–3 paragraphs, depending on how much you have to say. The description should come early in the paper, but purpose and audience could also work later.

Convention #1 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick one convention (common feature) of the genre to analyze.
  • Describe the convention and use evidence from your samples to support your point.
  • Analyze how this convention makes the genre effective (or ineffective) for its purpose.

Convention #2 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick another convention to analyze.
  • Describe it and use evidence from your samples.
  • Analyze its effectiveness.

Convention #3 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick a third convention to analyze.
  • Describe it and use evidence from your samples.
  • Analyze its effectiveness.

How the Genre Functions in the Community (1–2 paragraphs)

Pick one or two of the following approaches (or come up with your own):

  • Context & Circulation: How and where is this genre used? Formal or informal? How is it shared?
  • Interaction: How do community members interact with this genre? What do comments, sharing, and reactions tell us about the community?
  • Values & Norms: What does this genre reveal about the community's values, goals, or expectations?
  • Broader Implications: What does this analysis suggest about how genres function in communities more generally?
  • Something else: If you have another angle on how the genre and community relate, go for it.
Conclusion

Conclusion (1 paragraph)

  • Summarize your key findings from the analysis.
  • Restate how the genre contributes to the goals and functioning of the discourse community.
