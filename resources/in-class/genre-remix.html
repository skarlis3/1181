Genre Remix: Recipe Edition

In-Class Team Activity

Step 1: Genre Detective

Open the recipe sample and study it with your team. What conventions do you notice? Think about:

  • Structure and organization (sections, order of information)
  • Formatting (how ingredients are listed, numbered steps, headings)
  • Tone and word choice (what does it "sound" like?)
  • Visual and design elements (images, layout, typography, callout boxes)

As a team, list every convention you can identify.

Open Recipe Sample →

Step 2: Pull Up More Samples

Look up a few real recipes online (AllRecipes, food blogs, etc.) as a team. Compare them to the sample. What conventions are consistent across recipes? What varies? Add to your list.

Step 3: Plan Your Remix

As a team, choose a "recipe" topic that is not food. Some ideas:

Recipe for a Perfect Nap How to Make a Best Friend Recipe for Procrastination Recipe for the Perfect Playlist Recipe for Surviving Finals Week

Work together to figure out: What are the "ingredients"? What are the "steps"? What's the prep time and yield? What would the headnote sound like? What image would go with it?

Step 4: Design & Write It

Using a blank piece of paper, design and write your recipe as a team. This should look and sound like a real recipe — use the conventions you identified.

  • Include all the parts: title, headnote, time/yield info, ingredients, steps, a tip
  • Write in the tone and style of a recipe (imperative verbs, specific measurements, etc.)
  • Design the layout — where does each section go on the page?
  • Use boxes with labels like "picture of a cozy blanket" as placeholders for visual elements

Step 5: Gallery Walk

When your team is done, put your recipe up on the wall. We'll walk around and check out everyone's remixes.

The point of this activity: When you remix your Project 1 essay into a new genre, you'll need to do exactly what you just did here — identify the conventions of your target genre, then make deliberate choices about structure, tone, formatting, and design to match.
