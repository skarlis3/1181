In-Class Activity: Genre Detective & Remix

Below is a recipe page. With your team:

  1. Genre Detective: Study the recipe below. What conventions do you notice? Think about structure, formatting, tone, word choice, visual elements, and layout. List as many as you can on your shared doc or paper.
  2. Remix It: As a team, write a "recipe" for something that is not food. Use the conventions you identified to make it look and sound like a real recipe. Some ideas: Recipe for a Perfect Nap, How to Make a Best Friend, Recipe for Procrastination, Recipe for the Perfect Playlist, Recipe for Surviving Finals Week.
  3. Debrief: What choices did you have to make to fit your topic into this genre's conventions? What was easy to translate? What was tricky?

Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

By the King Arthur Baking Team

These homemade chocolate chip cookies are packed with chips and loaded with buttery flavor. They walk the line nicely between crunchy and chewy: the edges are crisp, while the center is perfectly "bendy." This is the kind of cookie that disappears from the cooling rack before you can even put them in a container. Fair warning: you'll want to double the batch.

Prep Time 12 min
Cook Time 12 min
Total Time 36 min
Yield 36 cookies

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup light brown sugar, packed
  • 2/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 8 tablespoons (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup vegetable shortening
  • 3/4 teaspoon table salt
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cider or white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Lightly grease two baking sheets or line them with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sugars, butter, shortening, salt, vanilla, almond extract (if using), vinegar, and baking soda. Beat until smooth and creamy, about 2 minutes.
  3. Add the egg and beat until well combined. Scrape down the sides of the bowl.
  4. Mix in the flour on low speed until just incorporated. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  5. Scoop dough into 1¼-inch balls and place on the prepared baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  6. For an extra touch, sprinkle a few flakes of sea salt on top of each cookie before baking.
  7. Bake for 11 to 12 minutes, until the edges are golden brown and the tops are light golden. The centers may look slightly underdone — that's what you want.
  8. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire cooling rack.
Baker's Tip: Don't overmix the dough after adding the flour — this keeps the cookies tender instead of tough. And resist the urge to overbake! They'll firm up as they cool. Pull them out when they still look a little soft in the middle.
